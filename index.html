Agentic AI for Municipal Workflows

What Changed Recently

Major enterprise platforms are moving from "assistants" to task-executing agents embedded in workflow systems. ServiceNow and Anthropic announced a partnership to build AI-powered applications, with Claude powering ServiceNow's "Build Agent" capabilities for agentic workflows.

Why the Board Should Care

Gartner forecasts 40% of enterprise apps will feature task-specific AI agents by 2026 (from <5% in 2025). This implies near-term vendor offerings and pressure to adopt. However, Gartner also warns that over 40% of agentic AI projects may be scrapped due to "agent washing" and governance failures‚Äîmaking bounded, auditable approaches critical.

Austin Pilot Angle

"Bounded agents" that can take actions only through approved tools/APIs:

  • Draft-and-route permits
  • Reconcile invoice exceptions
  • Pre-triage 311 calls to correct department queue
  • Generate inspection checklists

All with human approval + audit logs.

Risk Focus

Transparency, indeterminism, and permissioning boundaries must be addressed. High cancellation rates signal the need for careful governance from day one.

Transportation üöó

Autonomous Mobility Integration

What's Real

Uber launched "Waymo on Uber" in Austin on March 4, 2025. Riders can now be matched to fully autonomous Waymo vehicles directly in the Uber app. This is not a pilot‚Äîit's a commercial deployment operating today.

Why the Board Should Care

New data shows robotaxis are becoming competitive on price and wait time in mature markets. This accelerates adoption curves and increases city touchpoints: curb management, incident response, accessibility compliance, equity considerations, and enforcement protocols. Operational integration is now the bottleneck‚Äînot the technology.

Austin Pilot Angle

Create an AV operations playbook:

  • Designated curb management zones for pickup/dropoff
  • Data sharing agreements with AV operators
  • Emergency interaction procedures (first responders + AVs)
  • Standardized incident reporting protocols

Run a limited "smart curb" pilot in downtown and key corridors to test operational frameworks before broader deployment.

Public Safety üöÅ

Drone-as-First-Responder (DFR) + BVLOS

What Changed

The FAA published a Notice of Proposed Rulemaking (NPRM) to normalize BVLOS operations in August 2025, moving beyond one-off waivers toward routine authorization. The comment period was reopened in January 2026, indicating active rule evolution and strong industry engagement.

Why the Board Should Care

Police departments nationwide are rapidly adopting drones as first responders, with programs reporting arrival times measured in minutes rather than the typical 7+ minute response. However, this rapid adoption is triggering real privacy debates that cities must govern explicitly through transparent policies.

Austin Pilot Angle

A narrow, policy-forward DFR pilot for:

  • Missing person search
  • Fire situational awareness
  • Flood reconnaissance

With explicit governance: flight logging, data retention limits, camera-off-in-transit defaults, and public transparency dashboards.

Texas Context

Prosper launched DFR (2025); Laredo expanded its first-responder drone program. The FAA's BVLOS rulemaking is an external accelerant‚ÄîAustin should position its governance framework now before federal rules finalize.

Vision Zero üö¶

Edge-AI Traffic Safety Stack

What's Real in Austin

Austin's Smart Mobility Office is piloting NTT's Smart City Platform for real-time detection and notification‚Äîwrong-way driving incidents, vehicle counts, and traffic volumes. Additionally, Austin has deployed a connected-vehicle pedestrian crossing alert pilot using TAPCO/Siemens V2X technology that alerts approaching vehicles when pedestrians press crosswalk buttons.

Why the Board Should Care

This is "AI where it matters": situational awareness + faster response on known high-severity events (wrong-way driving, red-light running, pedestrian conflicts)‚Äîwithout being a resident-facing chatbot. These are pragmatic, measurable Vision Zero interventions with clear safety outcomes.

Austin Pilot Angle

  • Scale edge-AI detection to Highest Injury Network intersections
  • Evaluate "closed-loop" workflows: detection ‚Üí dispatch/notification ‚Üí after-action analytics
  • Expand V2X pedestrian crosswalk warnings to high-pedestrian corridors
  • Integrate with existing traffic management center operations
Utilities üíß

Predictive Water Maintenance

What's Real

Utilities are piloting acoustic "loggers" placed in fire hydrants that "listen" for leaks and alert crews before breaks become catastrophic. GovTech documented a Colorado utility pilot using AI-powered acoustic sensors, and Castle Rock is using similar AI models to detect underground leaks before they surface.

Why the Board Should Care

High-ROI, measurable outcomes:

  • Reduced non-revenue water (lost to undetected leaks)
  • Fewer catastrophic main breaks and emergency repairs
  • Optimized crew dispatch based on leak severity
  • Extended infrastructure lifespan through proactive maintenance

Austin Pilot Angle

Target a bounded pressure zone with known break history. Integrate leak alerts directly into Austin Water's work order system. Measure success via: gallons saved, avoided emergency breaks, mean time to repair.

Regional Digital Twins

What's Emerging

Regional coalitions are pushing digital twins to address traffic, development, climate, and cross-jurisdiction coordination. Phoenix is planning a regional digital twin to tackle cross-jurisdictional challenges across the metro area‚Äîa model Austin could adapt for the Central Texas region.

Why the Board Should Care

Digital twins become the "integration layer" for sensors, asset condition, permitting, and scenario planning (flood/heat/evacuation). They reduce friction between departments.

Austin Pilot Angle

Start with a single district (downtown, major corridor, or flood-prone watershed) and tight use-cases:

  • Capital planning conflict detection
  • Public-facing 3D visualization
  • Emergency routing scenarios
Data & Privacy üîê

Privacy-Enhancing Cryptography (PEC)

What It Is

NIST's Privacy-Enhancing Cryptography (PEC) program highlights techniques that allow analysis/linkage over sensitive datasets while reducing exposure of personal data. As explained in a NIST workshop on PEC for government, key techniques include:

  • Secure multi-party computation
  • Homomorphic encryption
  • Zero-knowledge proofs
  • Private set intersection

Why the Board Should Care

"Force multiplier" for evidence-based policy in health, housing, and homelessness while improving privacy posture. Reduces the need for broad data pooling.

Austin Pilot Angle

A small PEC proof-of-concept for a single join/use-case (e.g., "program overlap counts" or "eligibility signals") with strict data minimization and independent privacy review.

Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC)

What's Real

NIST finalized three PQC standards in August 2024 (FIPS 203/204/205) and continues the standardization program. This is no longer speculative research.

Why the Board Should Care

Long-lived municipal systems have long upgrade cycles:

  • PKI and VPN infrastructure
  • Identity systems
  • Critical infrastructure communications
  • Archival records

"Harvest now, decrypt later" is the strategic risk.

Action Signal

CISA has published guidance on product categories where PQC-standard implementations are available and issued a joint report with NSA and NIST on post-quantum cryptography migration. Use these resources to drive procurement requirements and vendor roadmaps.
