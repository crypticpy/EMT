AI & Automation ü§ñ

Agentic AI for Municipal Workflows

What Changed Recently

Major enterprise platforms are moving from "assistants" to task-executing agents embedded in workflow systems. ServiceNow is deepening Claude integration to power ServiceNow agents and "Build Agent" capabilities.

Why the Board Should Care

Gartner forecasts a rapid shift ‚Äî 40% of enterprise apps featuring task-specific AI agents by 2026 (from <5% in 2025). This implies near-term vendor offerings and pressure to adopt.

Austin Pilot Angle

"Bounded agents" that can take actions only through approved tools/APIs:

  • Draft-and-route permits
  • Reconcile invoice exceptions
  • Pre-triage 311 calls to correct department queue
  • Generate inspection checklists

All with human approval + audit logs.

Risk Focus

Transparency, indeterminism, and permissioning boundaries must be addressed.

Transportation üöó

Autonomous Mobility Integration

What's Real

Uber publicly launched "Waymo on Uber" in Austin on March 4, 2025. Riders can now be matched to Waymo AVs directly in the Uber app.

Why the Board Should Care

Robotaxis are trending toward practical competitiveness on price and wait time in mature markets. This accelerates adoption and increases city touchpoints: curb management, incidents, accessibility, equity, and enforcement.

Austin Pilot Angle

Create an AV operations playbook:

  • Curb management zones
  • Data sharing agreements
  • Emergency interaction procedures
  • Incident reporting protocols

Run a limited "smart curb" pilot in downtown and key corridors.

Public Safety üöÅ

Drone-as-First-Responder (DFR) + BVLOS

What Changed

FAA is actively pushing BVLOS (beyond visual line of sight) normalization via proposed rulemaking and programs like BEYOND, aiming to move beyond one-off waivers.

Why the Board Should Care

DFR programs have scaled quickly with widespread adoption and very fast scene arrival times. Real privacy debates require cities to govern explicitly.

Austin Pilot Angle

A narrow, policy-forward DFR pilot for:

  • Missing person search
  • Fire situational awareness
  • Flood reconnaissance

With explicit governance: flight logging, retention limits, camera-off-in-transit defaults, and public transparency.

Texas Context

Prosper launched DFR (2025); Laredo expanded first-responder drone program. AP reports U.S. proposed streamlining long-distance/BVLOS operations ‚Äî an external accelerant to plan for now.

Edge-AI Traffic Safety Stack

What's Real in Austin

Austin's Smart Mobility Office is piloting NTT's Smart City Platform for real-time detection and notification ‚Äî wrong-way driving incidents, vehicle counts, and traffic volumes.

Why the Board Should Care

This is "AI where it matters": situational awareness + faster response on known high-severity events (wrong-way driving, red-light running, pedestrian conflicts) ‚Äî without being a resident-facing chatbot.

Austin Pilot Angle

  • Scale to Highest Injury Network intersections
  • Evaluate "closed-loop" workflows: detection ‚Üí dispatch/notification ‚Üí after-action analytics
  • Connected pedestrian crosswalk pilot: vehicle alerts when button is pressed
Utilities üíß

Predictive Water Maintenance

What's Real

Utilities are piloting acoustic "loggers" placed in fire hydrants that "listen" for leaks and alert crews. Example: a Colorado utility pilot documented by GovTech.

Why the Board Should Care

High-ROI, measurable outcomes:

  • Reduced non-revenue water
  • Fewer catastrophic main breaks
  • Optimized crew dispatch

Austin Pilot Angle

Target a bounded pressure zone with known break history. Integrate leak alerts directly into the work order system. Measure: gallons saved, avoided breaks, response time.

Regional Digital Twins

What's Emerging

Regional coalitions are pushing digital twins to address traffic, development, climate, and cross-jurisdiction coordination. Example: Phoenix region effort.

Why the Board Should Care

Digital twins become the "integration layer" for sensors, asset condition, permitting, and scenario planning (flood/heat/evacuation). They reduce friction between departments.

Austin Pilot Angle

Start with a single district (downtown, major corridor, or flood-prone watershed) and tight use-cases:

  • Capital planning conflict detection
  • Public-facing 3D visualization
  • Emergency routing scenarios
Data & Privacy üîê

Privacy-Enhancing Cryptography (PEC)

What It Is

NIST's PEC work highlights techniques that allow analysis/linkage over sensitive datasets while reducing exposure of personal data:

  • Secure multi-party computation
  • Homomorphic encryption
  • Zero-knowledge proofs
  • Private set intersection

Why the Board Should Care

"Force multiplier" for evidence-based policy in health, housing, and homelessness while improving privacy posture. Reduces the need for broad data pooling.

Austin Pilot Angle

A small PEC proof-of-concept for a single join/use-case (e.g., "program overlap counts" or "eligibility signals") with strict data minimization and independent privacy review.

Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC)

What's Real

NIST finalized three PQC standards in 2024 (FIPS 203/204/205) and continues the standardization program. This is no longer speculative research.

Why the Board Should Care

Long-lived municipal systems have long upgrade cycles:

  • PKI and VPN infrastructure
  • Identity systems
  • Critical infrastructure communications
  • Archival records

"Harvest now, decrypt later" is the strategic risk.

Action Signal

CISA published guidance on product categories where PQC-standard implementations are available. Use this to drive procurement requirements and vendor roadmaps.
